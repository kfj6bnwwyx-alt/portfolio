Privacy

Larder & List is designed to collect as little data as possible. This page explains, in plain English, what the app touches and why.

What the app stores

  • Your grocery items, recipes, and preferences are stored in your iCloud private database. They sync between your own devices through Apple's CloudKit service, not through our servers.
  • When you share your household with another person, your data is shared with their iCloud account through Apple's CloudKit sharing. You can stop sharing at any time from Settings.
  • If you import a recipe from the web, the parsed text and image are stored in your iCloud database. We do not keep a copy.
  • If you scan a recipe with your camera, the captured images are processed on-device and never leave your phone.

What we collect

Nothing.

  • We do not run analytics.
  • We do not collect crash reports.
  • We do not have accounts, passwords, or servers that see your data.
  • We cannot read your grocery list or your recipes. Only Apple (briefly, in transit) and whoever you've shared your household with can see them.

Permissions the app requests

iCloud
To sync your data between your devices and with a shared household.
Camera
Only when you tap “Scan Recipe.” Images are processed on-device using Apple's Vision framework, then discarded.
Photo Library
Only when you choose a photo for a recipe.

You can deny any of these permissions and the app will still work, minus those specific features.

If you contact us

If you email us at brent.brooks@gmail.com, we'll have the email address you sent from and whatever you wrote. We use it to reply, and that's it.

Children

The app is rated 4+ and appropriate for all ages. It does not collect personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top and note the change in the next app update's release notes.
